What I've been up to at Etsy

Here's a selection of work I've been involved with at Etsy. As with most companies, it's really hard for me to take sole credit for any of this work as I have been on some extremely talented teams.

Currently, we are in the midst of testing and iterating a responsive version of search. As we made huge changes to how search feels and works, we broke up this work into sections and subsections to make sure we understood the impact being made.

One of the largest challenges Etsy faces is that there are 38 million items for sale on the site. And because of the type of content on Etsy, and that sellers are the ones listing items, a huge amount of these items are unique. I spent a good chunk of my time understanding the difference between low, medium, and high intent queries and understanding how we could have different and more tailored experiences in each case.

One example of a low intent query is the following design, where the visual treatment is still in flux, but the product side has proved to be a big win. The short summary is that when a user enters a query, we run a few calculations about the diversity of the result set between categories to determine the intent of the search. If it's proven to be low intent, we show a group of selected subcategories that help the user dive in further and help them narrow down their search. A teammate of mine wrote an in-depth summary of this technique you can read about here.
